Climate Change Impact

Climate change represents one of the most pressing challenges of our time, with far-reaching consequences across the globe. The evidence is overwhelming and the impacts are accelerating.

Key Statistics:

  • • 216 million climate migrants expected by 2050
  • • Global temperature has risen 1.1°C since pre-industrial times
  • • Sea levels rising at 3.3mm per year
  • • Arctic ice declining at 13% per decade

Primary Causes:

Industrial emissions (25%)
Transportation (14%)
Deforestation (11%)
Food systems (24%)

Rising Global Temperatures

Global temperatures are rising at an unprecedented rate, triggering cascading effects throughout Earth's climate system and fundamentally altering weather patterns worldwide.

Temperature Trends:

  • • 2023 was the hottest year on record globally
  • • Last decade was the warmest in 125,000 years
  • • Arctic warming twice as fast as global average
  • • Ocean temperatures rising to record levels

Consequences in ASEAN:

Extreme Weather

More frequent typhoons, droughts, and flooding events

Agriculture Impact

Reduced crop yields and shifting growing seasons

Sea Level Rise

Coastal flooding threatens 25% of ASEAN population

Food Waste Crisis

Food waste is responsible for approximately 6% of global greenhouse gas emissions, making it a critical area for climate action. The problem spans the entire food supply chain.

Global Food Waste Facts:

  • • 1.3 billion tons of food wasted annually
  • • Could feed 3 billion people
  • • 8-10% of global greenhouse gas emissions
  • • $1 trillion economic loss per year
